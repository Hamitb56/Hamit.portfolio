Modeling Criminal Systems

📄 View Full Article

This interdisciplinary research treats the process of burglary as a dynamic, controllable system rather than a random event. By applying Data Mining techniques, a dataset was constructed from over 100 real-world samples. The project demonstrates the power of Linear Regression and Control Theory in quantifying human behavior.

The Dataset: 100+ Samples

The following parameters were analyzed for predictive accuracy:

  • Offender Profile: Age, experience, and strategic planning.
  • Property Characteristics: Security systems, house type, and lock mechanisms.
  • Environmental Factors: Location, witness presence, and timing.
  • Victim Profile: Income level and behavioral habits.

Mathematical Modeling & Compensator Design

Using MATLAB, I designed classical control compensators to analyze system efficiency:

  • Linear Regression: Establishing baseline relationships between variables.
  • Lead Compensator: Increasing system response speed and time optimization.
  • Lag Compensator: Reducing steady-state error (damping ratio ζ = 0.7).
  • Root Locus Analysis: Ensuring stability under varying gain conditions.
